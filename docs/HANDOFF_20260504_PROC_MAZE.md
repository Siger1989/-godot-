## Critical Rules

- Plain rectangular rooms must stay below 40 percent.
- Each large procedural map needs real long corridors, L-shaped paths, recognizable rooms, hubs, large-room internal structures, local loops, and dead ends.
- Current proc-maze cell size is `2.5m`. Width tiers are `narrow_corridor=1 cell`, `normal_corridor=2 cells`, `normal_room=3 cells`, and `large_room/hub_room=4 cells`.
- Corridors must be visibly narrower than normal rooms; long corridors must have a stronger length/width ratio than rooms.
- Do not use normal rectangular room modules to fake corridors.
- Corridor side-door counts must stay controlled through graph degree validation.
- Proc-maze lamps are optional. Narrow corridors, L-turn corridors, T junctions, offset corridors, and short non-long corridor modules may be intentionally unlit.
- Ceiling light panels must fit inside the owner occupied-cell footprint and must not overlap boundary walls or internal partitions.
- Long rectangular ceiling-light panels must be backed by distributed real light sources along the long axis, not one full-strength center point.
- Door frames must not open directly into an abrupt wall or full-height internal partition. Doorway reveal clearance is generated from opening data and validates with `has_door_reveal_blocker=false`.
- Non-rectangular modules must use `shape_cells`.
- Validators and builders must use occupied cells, not only bounding rectangles.
- Doors connect connector-to-connector only. No door-to-wall saving.
- Do not use negative scale, mirror scale, or stretched wall hacks.
- Do not add `if Room_A/Room_B/...` logic.
- Visual experiments should be made on copied experiment scenes before touching accepted base scenes.
- Texture edits should go through `start_texture_tool.bat` when possible. The tool supports base albedo snapshots plus random overlay layers for grime/stain workflows; layer assets are stored under `materials/textures/texture_tool_layers/`.
